What are Quadratic Equations?

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree 2. This means that the highest power of the variable (usually x) is 2. Quadratic equations appear frequently in mathematics and have many real-world applications, from physics to economics.

Definition

A quadratic equation in one variable is an equation that can be written in the standard form:

ax² + bx + c = 0

Where a, b, and c are constants, and a ≠ 0.
